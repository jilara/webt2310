We are Going to go on an adventure!

Jacksonville, Florida, is a vibrant city with something to offer everyone. Nestled on the Atlantic coast of northeastern Florida, Jacksonville boasts stunning beaches, lush parks, a thriving arts and culture scene, and a rich history. History buffs can explore the city's rich past at the Jacksonville Museum of Modern Art, which houses an impressive collection of contemporary art, or the Cummer Museum of Art & Gardens, which features a stunning collection of European and American art. Nature lovers can hike or bike through the Timucuan Ecological and Historic Preserve, a sprawling 23,000-acre park with diverse ecosystems, or kayak through the pristine waters of the St. Johns River, the longest river in Florida. Jacksonville is also a great place to enjoy the outdoors. The city has 22 miles of beaches, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing. There are also numerous parks and gardens, including the Jacksonville Arboretum and Gardens, which features a variety of plants and flowers from around the world. For those seeking a taste of the local flavor, Jacksonville offers a diverse culinary scene, from fresh seafood to mouthwatering barbecue. The city is also home to a number of breweries and distilleries, perfect for those who enjoy sampling local craft drinks.

Haven for History Buffs

Jacksonville is a haven for history buffs, offering a wealth of opportunities to delve into the city's fascinating past. History comes alive at the acclaimed Jacksonville Museum of Modern Art (JMOMA), where visitors can immerse themselves in a captivating collection of contemporary art that chronicles Jacksonville's evolution and cultural tapestry.The museum's extensive collection spans over 7,000 works, encompassing a diverse range of media, including paintings, sculptures, photographs, and mixed-media installations. Visitors can embark on a journey through time, tracing the city's transformation from a small frontier outpost to the bustling metropolis it is today. JMOMA's galleries showcase the works of renowned artists who have captured Jacksonville's essence, including John Marin, Guy Carleton Wiggins, and William Merritt Chase. These artistic masterpieces provide a glimpse into the city's vibrant history, from its early days as a Native American settlement to its emergence as a major railroad hub and commercial center.

A Breathe of Fresh Air

For those seeking a tranquil escape amidst nature's embrace, the Jacksonville Arboretum and Gardens (JAG) is a must-visit destination. Spread across 127 acres, JAG boasts an impressive collection of plants and flowers from around the globe, providing a serene oasis in the heart of the city. Visitors can wander through the arboretum's meticulously landscaped gardens, admiring the vibrant blooms of the rose garden, the delicate beauty of the Japanese garden, and the lush greenery of the native plant collection. The arboretum also features a variety of educational exhibits, allowing visitors to learn about the diverse ecosystems of Florida and the importance of conservation. For those seeking a more adventurous encounter with nature, Little Talbot Island State Park offers a gateway to a pristine coastal wilderness. Located just east of downtown Jacksonville, this barrier island park encompasses over 5,000 acres of undeveloped beaches, dunes, and maritime forests.

For the Foodiesssss

Jacksonville's culinary scene is as diverse and vibrant as the city itself, offering a tantalizing array of flavors and dining experiences to satisfy every palate. From fresh seafood caught off the coast to mouthwatering barbecue, Jacksonville's restaurants cater to a wide range of culinary tastes and preferences. Seafood lovers will delight in the city's abundance of fresh seafood, with local restaurants offering a variety of dishes prepared with the day's catch. From succulent shrimp and scallops to flaky whitefish and hearty crab cakes, Jacksonville's seafood scene showcases the region's culinary heritage and the bounty of the Atlantic Ocean. Those seeking a taste of Southern comfort food will find Jacksonville's barbecue scene equally satisfying. Slow-smoked meats, tangy sauces, and savory side dishes are the hallmark of Jacksonville's barbecue, with renowned smokehouses and pitmasters enticing patrons with their irresistible aromas and mouthwatering flavors.

Let's Dive In

Jacksonville boasts a stunning 22 miles of coastline, offering a variety of beaches to suit every taste and preference. Whether you're seeking lively beachfront activities, tranquil sunbathing spots, or secluded coves for a romantic getaway, Jacksonville's beaches have something for everyone. For those seeking a vibrant and energetic beach experience, Jacksonville Beach is the perfect destination. This bustling seaside town offers a wide array of activities from swimming, sunbathing, and surfing to beach volleyball, kayaking, and paddleboarding. The beach itself is lined with shops, restaurants, and bars, providing endless opportunities for entertainment and relaxation. For those seeking a more laid-back beach experience, Neptune Beach offers a tranquil escape. This charming coastal community boasts a wide stretch of pristine sand, perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and leisurely walks along the shore. The beach is also home to a variety of surf schools, offering lessons for beginners and experienced surfers alike.
